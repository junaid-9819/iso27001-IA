Day 1: IS Governance and Organizational Controls

This guide provides a comprehensive set of questions and a structured approach for Day 1 of your ISO 27001:2022 internal audit. The focus today is on assessing leadership commitment, policy framework, and key organizational controls.

Controls Covered: A.5.1, A.5.2, A.5.4, A.5.7, A.5.9, A.5.12, A.5.15, A.5.18 (8 key controls, expandable if time allows).

Attendees: Top management, ISMS owner, policy/process owners.

Opening Meeting (30-60 minutes)

Purpose: Introduce the audit, clarify objectives, confirm logistics, and build rapport with the client team.

Attendees: Top management, ISMS owner, key stakeholders (e.g., department heads), audit coordinator.

Opening Meeting Questions/Agenda Points

  1. Introduction:
    • "Can you confirm your role and involvement in the ISMS for introductions?"
    • "Are there any recent changes to the organization or ISMS I should be aware of?"
  2. Audit Objectives:
    • "I'm here to assess your ISMS against ISO 27001:2022, starting with governance and organizational controls today. Does this align with your expectations?"
    • "Are there specific areas you'd like me to prioritize or concerns you want addressed?"
  3. Scope and Schedule:
    • "We're covering governance, policies, and roles today, as per the schedule sent. Does this work for your team?"
    • "Are the listed personnel available today, and are there any timing constraints?"
  4. Evidence and Access:
    • "I'll need policies, meeting minutes, and asset inventories today. Are these ready, and who's my point of contact for them?"
    • "Will I have access to documentation systems or need assistance retrieving records?"
  5. Process and Reporting:
    • "I'll document findings daily and share a draft report on Day 5. How would you prefer updates—daily briefings or just the final report?"
    • "Are there any confidentiality protocols I should follow for findings?"
  6. Closing:
    • "Any questions for me before we start?"
    • "Let's aim to kick off interviews in [e.g., 30 minutes]—does that work?"

Notes: Keep this conversational, take notes on responses, and confirm logistics (e.g., meeting rooms, contact person). End with a positive tone: "Looking forward to a productive week!"

Audit Questions for Day 1 (6-7 Hours)

Focus: Assess leadership commitment, policy framework, and key organizational controls (A.5.1–A.5.18).

A.5.1 Policies for Information Security

  • How are information security policies developed and approved by top management?
  • Can you show me the latest version of the security policy and evidence of management approval (e.g., signature, date)?
  • How are policies communicated to employees, contractors, and third parties?
  • Is there a process to review and update policies? How often does it happen?
  • Can I see records of the last policy review or communication (e.g., email, training session)?

A.5.2 Information Security Roles and Responsibilities

  • Who is the designated owner of the ISMS, and what are their responsibilities?
  • Are security roles and responsibilities clearly defined and documented for all relevant personnel?
  • Can you provide a RACI chart, org chart, or job descriptions showing these roles?
  • How are staff made aware of their security duties?
  • Is there evidence that these roles are understood and followed (e.g., signed acknowledgments)?

A.5.4 Management Responsibilities

  • How does top management demonstrate leadership and commitment to the ISMS?
  • Are information security objectives set and aligned with business goals? Can I see them?
  • What steps does management take to ensure resources (budget, staff, tools) are available for security?
  • Can you provide meeting minutes or memos showing management's involvement in security decisions?
  • How does management promote a security-aware culture across the organization?

A.5.7 Threat Intelligence

  • What process is in place to collect threat intelligence (e.g., feeds, reports, forums)?
  • How is this intelligence analyzed and integrated into risk management or decision-making?
  • Can you show me a recent threat intelligence report or example of its use?
  • Who is responsible for monitoring and acting on threat intelligence?
  • Is there evidence that threats identified have led to preventive actions?

A.5.9 Inventory of Information and Other Associated Assets

  • Is there a comprehensive, up-to-date inventory of assets (e.g., hardware, software, data)?
  • Who is responsible for maintaining and verifying the asset inventory?
  • Can I see the current inventory and evidence of its last update?
  • How are new assets added to the inventory, and how are obsolete ones removed?
  • Are asset owners assigned, and do they understand their security responsibilities?

A.5.12 Classification of Information

  • Is there a defined scheme for classifying information based on sensitivity (e.g., confidential, public)?
  • How is this classification applied to documents, data, and systems?
  • Can you show me examples of classified information (e.g., labeled files or emails)?
  • Who decides the classification level, and how are staff trained on it?
  • Is there evidence of periodic reviews to ensure classification remains appropriate?

A.5.15 Access Control

  • Is there a documented access control policy aligned with business needs?
  • How is access to systems and information restricted (e.g., role-based, need-to-know)?
  • Can you provide the access control policy and examples of its implementation (e.g., user permissions)?
  • How are exceptions to the policy handled, and are they documented?
  • Is there a process to monitor and audit access controls? Can I see audit results?

A.5.18 Access Rights

  • How are access rights assigned to users, and who approves them?
  • Are access rights reviewed periodically, and how often does this occur?
  • Can you show me a recent access rights review report or approval records?
  • What happens to access rights when someone changes roles or leaves the organization?
  • Is there evidence of timely revocation of access (e.g., logs, termination records)?

Additional Questions (Time Permitting)

If you finish early, expand to these related controls:

  • A.5.3 – Segregation of Duties:
    • How are conflicting duties separated (e.g., granting vs. approving access)?
    • Can I see examples of segregation in practice?
  • A.5.10 – Acceptable Use of Assets:
    • Is there an acceptable use policy for assets?
    • How is it enforced, and can I see it?

Execution Tips for Day 1

  • Time Management: Allocate ~45-60 minutes per control (including interviews and evidence review). Adjust based on responses.
  • Evidence Focus: Collect policies, minutes, inventories, and logs dated within the last 6-12 months to ensure relevance.
  • Approach: Start with open-ended questions ("How does…?"), then drill down with "Can I see…?" to verify.
  • Documentation: Use a checklist or notebook to track answers and evidence (e.g., "Policy v2.1, approved 01/15/2025").
  • Wrap-Up: End with a 15-minute debrief with the ISMS owner to confirm findings and preview Day 2.
