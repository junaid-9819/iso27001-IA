Day 3: Physical Controls and Supplier Management

This guide provides a comprehensive set of questions and a structured approach for Day 3 of your ISO 27001:2022 internal audit. Today's focus is on assessing physical security and supplier security management.

Controls Covered: A.7.1–A.7.14 (Physical, 14 controls) + A.5.19–A.5.22 (Organizational, 4 controls).

Attendees: Facilities staff, procurement team, ISMS owner.

Physical Controls (A.7)

A.7.1 Physical Security Perimeters

  • What physical barriers (e.g., fences, walls) protect secure areas like server rooms or offices?
  • Are these perimeters inspected regularly for vulnerabilities?
  • Can you show me a site map or layout of secure areas and inspection records?
  • How are breaches or weaknesses in perimeters addressed?
  • Are external entry points (e.g., gates) monitored or controlled?

A.7.2 Physical Entry Controls

  • What entry controls are in place (e.g., keycards, biometric scanners) for secure areas?
  • How are these controls maintained and monitored for failures?
  • Can I see access logs or test an entry system during my visit?
  • Who manages access credentials, and how are lost credentials handled?
  • Are visitors required to use entry controls, and how are they tracked?

A.7.3 Securing Offices, Rooms, and Facilities

  • How are internal workspaces (e.g., offices, file rooms) secured when unoccupied?
  • Are locks or access restrictions in place for sensitive areas?
  • Can I inspect a sample office or room during the site visit?
  • Who has keys or access to these areas, and is it documented?
  • How are unauthorized entries prevented or detected?

A.7.4 Physical Security Monitoring

  • What monitoring systems (e.g., CCTV, alarms) are used in secure areas?
  • How are recordings or alerts reviewed and acted upon?
  • Can you provide a sample CCTV log or alarm report from the last month?
  • Who is responsible for monitoring, and are they trained?
  • Are there gaps in coverage (e.g., blind spots) that need addressing?

A.7.5 Protecting Against Physical and Environmental Threats

  • What measures protect assets from environmental threats (e.g., fire extinguishers, flood barriers)?
  • Are these measures tested or maintained regularly?
  • Can I see maintenance or test records (e.g., fire drill log)?
  • How are risks like power outages or natural disasters mitigated?
  • Are critical equipment locations chosen to minimize exposure?

A.7.6 Working in Secure Areas

  • Are there procedures for working in secure areas (e.g., no photography)?
  • How are staff and visitors informed of these rules?
  • Can you show me the documented procedures and evidence of compliance?
  • Are violations of secure area rules tracked or penalized?
  • Is there oversight to ensure procedures are followed?

A.7.7 Clear Desk and Clear Screen

  • Is there a clear desk and clear screen policy to protect sensitive information?
  • How is compliance enforced (e.g., spot checks, reminders)?
  • Can I see the policy and evidence of its enforcement (e.g., check results)?
  • Are employees trained on this policy, and how?
  • Can I observe workspaces during the visit to verify adherence?

A.7.8 Equipment Siting and Protection

  • How is equipment (e.g., servers, workstations) positioned to reduce risks like theft or damage?
  • Are environmental factors (e.g., heat, humidity) controlled?
  • Can I see a server room or equipment layout during the visit?
  • Are risk assessments done for equipment placement?
  • How are unauthorized access attempts to equipment prevented?

A.7.9 Security of Assets Off-Premises

  • What security measures apply to assets used off-site (e.g., laptops, mobile devices)?
  • Are these assets tracked or monitored when outside secure areas?
  • Can you provide the off-site asset policy and an inventory of such assets?
  • How are off-site assets protected (e.g., encryption, locks)?
  • Are there examples of off-site asset security incidents and responses?

A.7.10 Storage Media

  • How is storage media (e.g., USBs, hard drives) containing sensitive data secured?
  • Are media stored in locked cabinets or safes when not in use?
  • Can I see the storage location and an inventory of media?
  • Who has access to storage media, and how is it controlled?
  • Is there a process for tracking media movement or loss?

A.7.11 Supporting Utilities

  • What utilities (e.g., power, cooling) support critical systems, and how are they protected?
  • Are backup systems (e.g., generators) in place and tested?
  • Can I see maintenance records or test results for utilities?
  • How are utility failures detected and addressed?
  • Are utility disruptions documented and reviewed?

A.7.12 Cabling Security

  • Are power and network cables protected from damage or interception?
  • How are cables concealed or secured (e.g., conduits, locked panels)?
  • Can I inspect cabling during the site visit?
  • Are cable vulnerabilities assessed or inspected?
  • Is there evidence of cabling maintenance or upgrades?

A.7.13 Equipment Maintenance

  • Is there a schedule for maintaining equipment (e.g., servers, HVAC)?
  • Who performs maintenance, and are they vetted?
  • Can you show me maintenance logs from the last 6 months?
  • How are maintenance issues prioritized and resolved?
  • Are security considerations part of maintenance planning?

A.7.14 Secure Disposal or Reuse of Equipment

  • What process ensures sensitive data is removed before equipment disposal or reuse?
  • Are secure wiping tools or methods used?
  • Can I see a disposal certificate or log from a recent disposal?
  • Who oversees the disposal process, and are they trained?
  • Is third-party disposal (if used) compliant with security requirements?

Supplier Management Controls (A.5.19-A.5.22)

A.5.19 Information Security in Supplier Relationships

  • Do supplier contracts include specific security requirements?
  • How are these requirements defined and agreed upon?
  • Can you provide a sample supplier contract with security clauses?
  • Are suppliers informed of their security obligations?
  • Is there evidence of supplier acknowledgment or compliance?

A.5.20 Addressing Information Security Within Supplier Agreements

  • How do you ensure suppliers meet security obligations in agreements?
  • Are supplier security practices monitored or audited?
  • Can I see a supplier audit report or monitoring record?
  • What happens if a supplier fails to comply with security terms?
  • Are there escalation processes for supplier security issues?

A.5.21 Managing Information Security in the ICT Supply Chain

  • How are security risks assessed across the ICT supply chain?
  • What controls are applied to mitigate supply chain risks?
  • Can you show me a supply chain risk assessment?
  • Are critical suppliers prioritized for security oversight?
  • Is there evidence of supply chain risk reviews?

A.5.22 Monitoring, Review, and Change Management of Supplier Services

  • How often are supplier services reviewed for security compliance?
  • Are changes to supplier services managed with security in mind?
  • Can I see a recent supplier review report or change log?
  • Who is responsible for supplier monitoring, and how are they trained?
  • Are supplier performance metrics tied to security?

Execution Tips for Day 3

  • Time Management: Allocate ~25-30 minutes per control (18 controls ≈ 7-8 hours, including travel between sites). Start with Physical controls, then shift to suppliers.
  • Evidence Focus: Collect logs, site plans, and contracts (recent, e.g., within 6-12 months). Take photos (with permission) during site visits for records.
  • Approach: Combine interviews with physical inspections—e.g., ask about A.7.2, then test an entry control. Use "Can I see…?" to verify supplier claims.
  • Site Visits: Plan the route in advance (e.g., server room, offices) and bring a checklist for observations.
  • Wrap-Up: Debrief with the facilities or procurement lead (15-20 minutes) to confirm findings and preview Day 4 (Technological Controls).
