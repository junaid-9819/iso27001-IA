Day 5: Technological Wrap-Up and Compliance

This guide provides a comprehensive set of questions and a structured approach for Day 5 of your ISO 27001:2022 internal audit. Today's focus is on completing technological controls assessment and reviewing compliance controls.

Controls Covered: A.8.21–A.8.34 (Technological, 14 controls) + A.5.31–A.5.37 (Organizational, 7 controls).

Attendees: IT developers, compliance officer, ISMS owner.

Remaining Technological Controls (A.8.21-A.8.34)

A.8.21 Security of Network Services

  • Are network services (e.g., VPNs, cloud services) configured securely?
  • How are service security risks assessed and mitigated?
  • Can you show me a service configuration or risk assessment?
  • Are service providers monitored for security compliance?
  • Is there evidence of network service testing?

A.8.22 Segregation of Networks

  • Are networks segmented based on risk or function (e.g., guest vs. internal)?
  • How is segmentation enforced (e.g., VLANs, firewalls)?
  • Can I see a network diagram showing segmentation?
  • Are segregation controls tested for effectiveness?
  • How are breaches of segregation detected?

A.8.23 Web Filtering

  • Is web filtering implemented to block malicious or unauthorized sites?
  • How are filtering policies defined and updated?
  • Can you provide a filtering policy or log of blocked sites?
  • Are filtering exceptions documented and approved?
  • How are staff informed of web usage rules?

A.8.24 Use of Cryptography

  • Where is cryptography applied (e.g., data at rest, in transit)?
  • Are encryption keys managed securely (e.g., rotation, storage)?
  • Can I see an encryption policy or key management log?
  • Are cryptographic controls tested for strength?
  • How are weak encryption methods identified and replaced?

A.8.25 Secure Development Life Cycle

  • Is there a secure SDLC policy for software development?
  • Are developers trained on secure coding practices?
  • Can you show me the SDLC policy and training records?
  • How are security requirements integrated into development?
  • Is the SDLC reviewed for effectiveness?

A.8.26 Application Security Requirements

  • Are security requirements defined for new or updated applications?
  • How are these requirements validated before deployment?
  • Can I see a requirements document for a recent project?
  • Who ensures security is included in app design?
  • Are deviations from requirements tracked?

A.8.27 Secure System Architecture and Engineering Principles

  • Are secure design principles applied to system architecture?
  • How is security built into system planning?
  • Can you provide architecture documentation with security features?
  • Are designs reviewed for security before implementation?
  • Is there evidence of architectural risk assessments?

A.8.28 Secure Coding

  • Are secure coding standards defined and followed?
  • Is code reviewed for security vulnerabilities?
  • Can I see coding guidelines and a review report?
  • Are tools (e.g., static analysis) used to check code?
  • How are coding flaws remediated?

A.8.29 Security Testing in Development and Acceptance

  • What security tests (e.g., penetration testing) are performed before deployment?
  • Are test results documented and acted upon?
  • Can I see a recent test report and remediation plan?
  • Who oversees security testing, and are they qualified?
  • Are critical issues fixed before release?

A.8.30 Outsourced Development

  • Are security requirements included in outsourced development contracts?
  • How is outsourced work monitored for compliance?
  • Can you show me a contract and monitoring evidence?
  • Are deliverables tested for security before acceptance?
  • How are outsourcing risks managed?

A.8.31 Separation of Development, Test, and Production Environments

  • Are development, test, and production environments separated?
  • How is data handled across these environments (e.g., anonymized in test)?
  • Can I see environment configs or separation policies?
  • Are unauthorized cross-environment accesses detected?
  • Is separation audited regularly?

A.8.32 Change Management

  • Is there a formal process for managing system changes?
  • Are changes approved and logged before implementation?
  • Can you provide a change management policy and recent change log?
  • How are security impacts of changes assessed?
  • Are failed changes tracked and reviewed?

A.8.33 Test Information

  • Is test data protected (e.g., anonymized, synthetic)?
  • How is test data secured against exposure?
  • Can I see a test data policy or example?
  • Are access controls applied to test environments?
  • Is test data disposal verified?

A.8.34 Protection of Information Systems During Audit Tests

  • How are systems protected during audit or security tests?
  • Are test risks assessed and mitigated?
  • Can you show me a test plan with protection measures?
  • Who ensures testing doesn't disrupt operations?
  • Is there evidence of secure test execution?

Compliance Controls (A.5.31-A.5.37)

A.5.31 Legal, Statutory, Regulatory, and Contractual Requirements

  • How are applicable legal and regulatory requirements identified?
  • Are these requirements documented and monitored?
  • Can I see a compliance register or legal assessment?
  • How are changes in requirements tracked?
  • Is compliance audited, and by whom?

A.5.32 Intellectual Property Rights

  • Are intellectual property (IP) rights protected (e.g., software, designs)?
  • How is IP compliance ensured?
  • Can you provide an IP policy or compliance evidence?
  • Are staff trained on IP responsibilities?
  • Are IP violations tracked?

A.5.33 Protection of Records

  • How are records protected from loss or unauthorized access?
  • Are retention and disposal policies followed?
  • Can I see a records management policy or storage example?
  • Who manages record security?
  • Are record breaches investigated?

A.5.34 Privacy and Protection of PII

  • Is personally identifiable information (PII) identified and safeguarded?
  • Are privacy policies aligned with regulations (e.g., GDPR)?
  • Can I see a PII inventory or privacy assessment?
  • How are privacy breaches handled?
  • Are staff trained on PII protection?

A.5.35 Independent Review of Information Security

  • When was the last independent ISMS review conducted?
  • What were the key findings, and are they addressed?
  • Can I see the review report or action plan?
  • Who commissions these reviews?
  • Are reviews scheduled regularly?

A.5.36 Compliance with Security Policies and Standards

  • How is compliance with security policies audited internally?
  • Are nonconformities tracked and resolved?
  • Can I see an internal audit report or nonconformity log?
  • Who oversees policy compliance?
  • Is compliance tied to performance metrics?

A.5.37 Documented Operating Procedures

  • Are security operating procedures documented and maintained?
  • How are they kept current and accessible?
  • Can I see a sample procedure (e.g., backup, access)?
  • Are staff trained on these procedures?
  • Is procedure adherence verified?

Closing Meeting (45-60 minutes)

Purpose: Summarize the audit, present preliminary findings, discuss next steps, and close on a positive note.

Attendees: Top management, ISMS owner, key stakeholders (e.g., IT, HR, facilities leads), audit coordinator.

Time: Scheduled for late Day 5 (e.g., 3:00-4:00 PM).

Closing Meeting Agenda/Questions

1. Introduction

  • "Thank you all for your support over the past 5 days. We've covered governance, people, physical, technological, and compliance controls."
  • "Today, I'll summarize key findings and outline next steps. Any opening remarks from your side?"

2. Audit Summary

  • "Day 1 showed [e.g., strong governance but gaps in policy updates]. Day 2 highlighted [e.g., good training, incident response needs work]. Day 3 confirmed [e.g., solid physical security, supplier oversight lacking]. Day 4 and 5 revealed [e.g., robust IT controls, compliance documentation incomplete]. Does this resonate?"
  • "Overall, I've audited ~77 controls. Strengths include [e.g., leadership commitment, endpoint security]. Areas for improvement are [e.g., incident testing, supplier audits]. Thoughts?"

3. Preliminary Findings

  • "I've noted [e.g., 5 major, 10 minor nonconformities]. Examples: [e.g., no recent vulnerability scans (A.8.8), incomplete PII protection (A.5.34)]. Agree with these observations?"
  • "Positive findings include [e.g., clear desk enforcement (A.7.7), effective MFA (A.8.5)]. Any surprises here?"

4. Next Steps

  • "I'll finalize the draft report by [e.g., April 20, 2025] and send it via [e.g., email]. When can you review it?"
  • "You'll need to address nonconformities for certification readiness. Who will coordinate the action plan?"
  • "Would you like a follow-up meeting to discuss the report?"

5. Feedback

  • "How did the audit process work for you? Any suggestions for future audits?"
  • "Anything else you'd like me to include in the report?"

6. Closing

  • "Thank you again for your cooperation. This has been a thorough review, and I'm confident you're on a strong path to compliance. Any final comments?"

Notes: Prepare a concise summary (e.g., 1-2 pages) of findings to share. Be diplomatic—highlight strengths while flagging gaps. Confirm report delivery details.

Execution Tips for Day 5

  • Time Management: Allocate ~15-20 minutes per control (21 controls ≈ 5-6 hours, including breaks). Finish auditing by midday to draft findings.
  • Evidence Focus: Collect test reports, compliance registers, and policies (recent, e.g., within 6-12 months). Request quick demos if needed.
  • Approach: Start with development controls (A.8.21–A.8.34), then shift to compliance (A.5.31–A.5.37). Use "Can I see…?" to verify.
  • Drafting: Spend 2-3 hours post-audit compiling notes into a draft report (scope, findings, recommendations).
  • Closing: Keep the meeting structured but open for discussion. End positively to maintain goodwill.
