Day 2: People Controls and Incident Management

This guide provides a comprehensive set of questions and a structured approach for Day 2 of your ISO 27001:2022 internal audit. Today's focus is on assessing human security controls and incident management processes.

Controls Covered: A.6.1–A.6.8 (People, 8 controls) + A.5.24–A.5.28 (Organizational, 5 controls).

Attendees: HR staff, random employees (for awareness checks), incident response team, ISMS owner.

People Controls (A.6)

A.6.1 Screening

  • What background checks are performed for new employees and contractors (e.g., criminal, references)?
  • Are screening requirements consistent across roles, or do they vary by sensitivity?
  • Can you show me a sample screening record (anonymized) and the screening policy?
  • How are third-party screenings (e.g., agency hires) verified?
  • Is there evidence of screening completion before access is granted?

A.6.2 Terms and Conditions of Employment

  • Do employment contracts include information security responsibilities?
  • How are these responsibilities communicated to employees at onboarding?
  • Can I see a sample contract or onboarding document with security clauses?
  • Are contractors and temporary staff bound by similar terms?
  • Is there evidence that staff acknowledge these terms (e.g., signed agreements)?

A.6.3 Information Security Awareness, Education, and Training

  • What security awareness training is provided to employees (e.g., phishing, policies)?
  • How frequently is training conducted, and is it mandatory for all staff?
  • Can you provide training materials and attendance records from the last session?
  • How do you measure training effectiveness (e.g., quizzes, feedback)?
  • Are new hires trained before accessing sensitive systems?

A.6.4 Disciplinary Process

  • Is there a formal disciplinary process for security policy violations?
  • How is it documented and communicated to staff?
  • Can I see the disciplinary policy and an anonymized example of its application?
  • Who is responsible for enforcing disciplinary actions?
  • Are violations tracked, and how are repeat offenders handled?

A.6.5 Responsibilities After Termination or Change of Employment

  • What process ensures access rights are revoked when someone leaves or changes roles?
  • Are company assets (e.g., laptops, badges) returned upon termination?
  • Can you show me an exit checklist or access revocation log from a recent termination?
  • How are delays in revocation prevented or addressed?
  • Do security obligations (e.g., confidentiality) extend post-employment, and how are they enforced?

A.6.6 Confidentiality or Non-Disclosure Agreements

  • Are confidentiality or NDAs required for employees and contractors?
  • How are these agreements managed and enforced?
  • Can I see a signed NDA template and a recent example?
  • Are third parties (e.g., vendors) also bound by NDAs?
  • Is there a process to address breaches of confidentiality?

A.6.7 Remote Working

  • Are security policies defined for remote work (e.g., VPN use, device security)?
  • How are remote workers trained on these policies?
  • Can you provide the remote work policy and evidence of its implementation?
  • How do you monitor compliance for remote devices or environments?
  • Are there examples of remote work security incidents and how they were handled?

A.6.8 Information Security Event Reporting

  • What process exists for staff to report security events or incidents?
  • How are employees encouraged to report (e.g., anonymous channels)?
  • Can you show me the reporting procedure and recent event reports?
  • Are staff trained on what constitutes a reportable event?
  • How are reported events escalated and tracked?

Incident Management Controls (A.5.24-A.5.28)

A.5.24 Information Security Incident Management Planning and Preparation

  • Is there a documented incident response plan, and is it approved?
  • How often is the plan reviewed or tested?
  • Can I see the current plan and results from the last test (e.g., tabletop exercise)?
  • Who is responsible for maintaining and executing the plan?
  • Are roles and resources clearly defined for incident response?

A.5.25 Assessment and Decision on Information Security Events

  • How are security events identified and assessed for severity?
  • Who decides whether an event becomes an incident requiring action?
  • Can I see a log of recent events and their assessments?
  • Are there defined criteria or thresholds for escalation?
  • Is there evidence of timely decision-making on events?

A.5.26 Response to Information Security Incidents

  • What steps are followed when responding to a security incident?
  • How are responses documented and communicated to stakeholders?
  • Can you provide an incident report from the last 6 months?
  • Are containment, eradication, and recovery processes defined?
  • Who coordinates the response, and how are they trained?

A.5.27 Learning from Information Security Incidents

  • How are lessons learned from incidents captured and shared?
  • Are improvements implemented based on these lessons?
  • Can I see a lessons-learned document or post-incident review?
  • Who is responsible for driving improvements post-incident?
  • Is there evidence that past incidents have reduced recurrence?

A.5.28 Collection of Evidence

  • What process ensures evidence is collected during incidents?
  • Is evidence preserved in a way that's legally admissible (e.g., chain of custody)?
  • Can I see an example of evidence collected from a past incident?
  • Are staff trained on evidence-handling procedures?
  • How is evidence stored and protected from tampering?

Execution Tips for Day 2

  • Time Management: Allocate ~30-45 minutes per control (13 controls ≈ 6.5-7 hours). Start with People controls (HR focus), then shift to incident management.
  • Evidence Focus: Request records like training logs, NDAs, and incident reports (dated within 6-12 months). Anonymize sensitive data as needed.
  • Approach: Use open-ended questions with HR/staff, then verify with "Can I see…?" Incident questions may need technical input—coordinate with the response team.
  • Engagement: Test awareness by asking random staff, "What would you do if you suspect a security issue?"
  • Wrap-Up: Debrief with the ISMS owner or incident team (15-20 minutes) to confirm findings and preview Day 3 (Physical Controls).
