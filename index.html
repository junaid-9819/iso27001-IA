ISO 27001:2022 5-Day Internal Audit Schedule

Planning a 5-day internal audit for ISO 27001:2022 that covers all four themes—Organizational (37 controls), People (8 controls), Physical (14 controls), and Technological (34 controls)—requires a structured schedule to ensure you address the major areas efficiently.

With 93 controls in total, it's impractical to audit every single one in depth in just 5 days, so this schedule prioritizes key controls within each theme, aligns them with IS Governance (your starting point), and ensures a balanced approach.

Audit Schedule Overview

Date Focus Area Key Activities Personnel Needed Evidence/Documents Requested
Day 1 IS Governance & Organizational Controls Interviews with leadership, policy review Top management, ISMS owner Security policies, asset inventory, meeting minutes
Day 2 People Controls & Incident Management HR/staff interviews, incident process review HR, staff reps, incident response team Training logs, exit checklists, incident reports
Day 3 Physical Controls & Supplier Management Site visits, supplier contract review Facilities staff, procurement team Access logs, site maps, supplier agreements
Day 4 Technological Controls (Core IT) IT interviews, system/log reviews IT team, system admins Device configs, malware scan results, network logs
Day 5 Technological Wrap-Up & Compliance Development/compliance review, report drafting IT developers, compliance officer Test results, privacy assessments, compliance register

Assumptions

  • You're auditing a medium-sized organization with a defined ISMS.
  • You have access to key personnel (e.g., management, IT, HR, facilities) and documentation.
  • The goal is to assess compliance, identify gaps, and provide actionable findings.
  • Each day includes a mix of themes to maintain momentum and cover critical controls.

Detailed Daily Schedule

Day 1: IS Governance and Organizational Controls

1

Objective: Establish the foundation by auditing IS Governance and key Organizational Controls (A.5.1–A.5.18), focusing on leadership, policies, and roles.

Time Allocation: 8 hours (interviews with management, policy review, documentation check).

Controls Covered: ~10 Organizational controls (prioritizing governance-related ones).

Coverage:

  1. A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security
  2. A.5.2 - Information Security Roles and Responsibilities
  3. A.5.4 - Management Responsibilities
  4. A.5.7 - Threat Intelligence
  5. A.5.9 - Inventory of Information and Assets
  6. A.5.12 - Classification of Information
  7. A.5.15 - Access Control
  8. A.5.18 - Access Rights

Activities: Interview top management, review policies, check asset registers, and document findings.

Day 2: People Controls and Incident Management (Organizational)

2

Objective: Assess human-related security and incident management processes (all 8 People controls, A.6.1–A.6.8, plus Organizational A.5.24–A.5.28).

Time Allocation: 8 hours (HR interviews, staff discussions, incident record review).

Controls Covered: 8 People controls + 5 Organizational controls.

Coverage:

  1. A.6.1 - Screening
  2. A.6.3 - Information Security Awareness
  3. A.6.5 - Responsibilities After Termination
  4. A.6.7 - Remote Working
  5. A.6.8 - Information Security Event Reporting
  6. A.5.24 - Incident Management Planning
  7. A.5.26 - Response to Information Security Incidents

Activities: Meet HR and staff, review training records, examine incident logs, and assess remote work practices.

Day 3: Physical Controls and Risk Management (Organizational)

3

Objective: Evaluate physical security and risk-related Organizational controls (all 14 Physical controls, A.7.1–A.7.14, plus A.5.19–A.5.22 for suppliers).

Time Allocation: 8 hours (site visits, facilities staff interviews, supplier contract review).

Controls Covered: 14 Physical controls + 4 Organizational controls.

Coverage:

  1. A.7.1 - Physical Security Perimeters
  2. A.7.2 - Physical Entry Controls
  3. A.7.5 - Protection Against Physical Threats
  4. A.7.7 - Clear Desk and Clear Screen
  5. A.7.14 - Secure Disposal of Equipment
  6. A.5.19 - Information Security in Supplier Relationships
  7. A.5.22 - Monitoring Supplier Services

Activities: Tour facilities, inspect physical controls, interview facilities staff, and review supplier agreements.

Day 4: Technological Controls (Core IT Security)

4

Objective: Assess critical Technological controls (A.8.1–A.8.20, focusing on endpoints, access, and networks).

Time Allocation: 8 hours (IT team interviews, system demos, log reviews).

Controls Covered: ~15 Technological controls (prioritizing operational security).

Coverage:

  1. A.8.1 - User Endpoint Devices
  2. A.8.2 - Privileged Access Rights
  3. A.8.5 - Secure Authentication
  4. A.8.7 - Protection Against Malware
  5. A.8.8 - Management of Technical Vulnerabilities
  6. A.8.15 - Logging
  7. A.8.20 - Networks

Activities: Work with IT staff, review system configurations, inspect logs, and test controls where possible.

Day 5: Technological Wrap-Up, Compliance, and Reporting

5

Objective: Finish Technological controls (A.8.21–A.8.34), cover compliance (A.5.31–A.5.37), and finalize findings.

Time Allocation: 8 hours (4 hours auditing, 4 hours analysis/reporting).

Controls Covered: ~14 Technological controls + 7 Organizational controls.

Coverage:

  1. A.8.25 - Secure Development Life Cycle
  2. A.8.29 - Security Testing in Development
  3. A.8.34 - Protection During Audit Tests
  4. A.5.31 - Legal and Regulatory Compliance
  5. A.5.34 - Privacy and Protection of PII
  6. A.5.36 - Compliance with Security Policies

Activities: Complete IT interviews, review compliance docs, analyze findings, and draft a preliminary report.

Summary of Coverage

  • Day 1: Organizational (10 controls) – Governance focus.
  • Day 2: People (8 controls) + Organizational (5 controls) – Human and incident focus.
  • Day 3: Physical (14 controls) + Organizational (4 controls) – Physical and supplier focus.
  • Day 4: Technological (15 controls) – Core IT security.
  • Day 5: Technological (14 controls) + Organizational (7 controls) – Development, compliance, and wrap-up.
  • Total: ~77 controls explicitly covered, with overlap ensuring major areas are addressed.
